Using CASAVA to extract raw fastq files from Illumina BCL files

Sep 18, 2012 by Owen
Here is how you can extract raw fastq files from Illumina BCL data. This will give you raw fastq files. No demultiplexing. Just the reads, and nothin’ but the reads. Read 1 is generally the forward read, Read 2 is generally the index read, and Read 3 is generally the reverse read. You might want to do this if you want to see the barcode read quality scores, which you may want to consider to do a more accurate demultiplexing.
